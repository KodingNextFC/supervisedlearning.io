This is supervised learning, hands-on: you draw a digit, tell the model what it is, and repeat. Once it has seen enough labeled examples, it can guess new drawings on its own.

Draw something and add a few training examples first.
What's happening here, step by step: Every time you draw a digit and click "Add as Training Example," you're giving the model a labeled example — a pair of (drawing, correct answer). That's the core of supervised learning. The model doesn't know anything on its own; it only learns the patterns you show it. The more examples you give per digit (and the more varied your handwriting styles are), the better it gets at recognizing new, unlabeled drawings.
1. Input (X) = the drawing
2. Label (y) = the digit you tell it
3. Train = store labeled pairs
4. Predict = guess y for a new X
